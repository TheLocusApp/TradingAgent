STRATEGY_NAME: DualCrossover

STRATEGY_DETAILS:
This is a short-term momentum trading strategy based on Exponential Moving Average (EMA) crossovers specifically designed for QQQ ETF on 5-minute timeframes.

KEY STRATEGY COMPONENTS:
- Instrument: QQQ (Invesco QQQ Trust tracking NASDAQ-100)
- Timeframe: 5-minute charts exclusively
- Primary Indicators: 8-period EMA and 13-period EMA
- Core Concept: Capturing short-term momentum shifts through EMA crossovers

REQUIRED INDICATORS:
1. 8-period Exponential Moving Average (EMA)
2. 13-period Exponential Moving Average (EMA)
3. Volume confirmation (recommended but not required)

ENTRY RULES:
LONG ENTRY:
- When the 8-period EMA crosses ABOVE the 13-period EMA
- Enter on the close of the candle where crossover occurs
- Confirm with bullish price action (closing above crossover point)

SHORT ENTRY:
- When the 8-period EMA crosses BELOW the 13-period EMA
- Enter on the close of the candle where crossover occurs
- Confirm with bearish price action (closing below crossover point)

EXIT RULES:
LONG POSITION EXIT:
- When the 8-period EMA crosses below the 13-period EMA
- Exit on the close of the crossover candle

SHORT POSITION EXIT:
- When the 8-period EMA crosses above the 13-period EMA
- Exit on the close of the crossover candle

RISK MANAGEMENT:
- Position sizing: Risk no more than 1-2% of account per trade
- Stop loss: Place 0.5-1% below/above recent swing low/high
- Time-based exit: Consider exiting if no significant movement occurs within 3-5 candles
- Avoid trading during first/last 30 minutes of market hours due to volatility
- Filter out low-volume periods (lunch hours, pre-market, after-hours)

STRATEGY ENHANCEMENTS:
- Add volume confirmation: Higher volume on crossover increases signal reliability
- Consider 20-period SMA as trend filter (only trade in direction of 20 SMA)
- Avoid trading during major economic news releases
- Monitor overall market sentiment and NASDAQ direction for confirmation

This strategy works best in trending markets and may produce whipsaws during ranging or choppy conditions. Regular monitoring and discipline in following crossover signals are essential for success.
