STRATEGY_NAME: DivergenceCatalyst

STRATEGY_DETAILS:
This strategy combines RSI divergence patterns with MACD confirmation signals specifically for SPY daily charts, creating a robust mean-reversion approach with momentum validation.

KEY COMPONENTS:
1. RSI (Relative Strength Index) - 14-period standard setting
2. MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) - 12,26,9 standard settings
3. SPY (SPDR S&P 500 ETF) daily timeframe exclusively
4. Divergence detection between price action and RSI
5. MACD histogram and signal line analysis

ENTRY RULES:
LONG ENTRY (Bullish Divergence):
- Price makes lower low while RSI makes higher low (bullish divergence)
- MACD histogram shows upward momentum (rising bars)
- MACD line crosses above signal line or shows bullish alignment
- Entry on next candle open after divergence confirmation

SHORT ENTRY (Bearish Divergence):
- Price makes higher high while RSI makes lower high (bearish divergence)
- MACD histogram shows downward momentum (falling bars)
- MACD line crosses below signal line or shows bearish alignment
- Entry on next candle open after divergence confirmation

EXIT RULES:
- Take profit: 2:1 risk-reward ratio minimum
- Stop loss: Recent swing low (long) or swing high (short)
- Alternative exit: RSI reaches overbought (70+) for longs or oversold (30-) for shorts
- Time-based exit: Close position after 5-10 trading days if target not reached

RISK MANAGEMENT:
- Maximum 2% risk per trade
- Position sizing based on stop loss distance
- No new entries during major economic events or Fed announcements
- Avoid trading during first/last 30 minutes of market hours
- Weekly correlation check with overall market trend

REQUIRED INDICATORS:
- RSI (14-period) - for divergence detection
- MACD (12,26,9) - for momentum confirmation
- Volume indicator (optional but recommended) - to confirm divergence strength

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS FOR SPY:
- Account for market hours (9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET)
- Consider overall market context and sector rotation
- Be aware of dividend dates and ex-dividend price adjustments
- Monitor VIX levels for market volatility context
- Align with broader S&P 500 technical structure
