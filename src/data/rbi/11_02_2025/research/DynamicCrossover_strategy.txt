STRATEGY_NAME: DynamicCrossover

STRATEGY_DETAILS:
This strategy implements a systematic approach using Simple Moving Average crossovers on SPY daily charts with specific entry, exit, and risk management rules.

KEY STRATEGY COMPONENTS:
- Primary Instrument: SPY (SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust)
- Timeframe: Daily charts
- Core Indicator: 20-period Simple Moving Average (SMA)
- Crossover-based entry/exit signals
- Trend-following methodology

REQUIRED INDICATORS:
- 20-period Simple Moving Average (SMA)
- Price action (Open, High, Low, Close)

ENTRY RULES:
LONG ENTRY:
- When price closes ABOVE the 20-period SMA
- Previous close must have been below the 20-period SMA
- Enter long position on next trading day's open

SHORT ENTRY:
- When price closes BELOW the 20-period SMA
- Previous close must have been above the 20-period SMA
- Enter short position on next trading day's open

EXIT RULES:
LONG EXIT:
- When price closes below the 20-period SMA
- Exit long position on next trading day's open

SHORT EXIT:
- When price closes above the 20-period SMA
- Exit short position on next trading day's open

RISK MANAGEMENT:
- Position sizing: Allocate no more than 2-5% of total capital per trade
- Stop loss: Set at 2% below entry price for long positions, 2% above entry price for short positions
- Maximum drawdown limit: Cease trading if account experiences 10% drawdown from peak
- Trade only during regular market hours (9:30 AM - 4:00 PM EST)
- Avoid trading during major economic announcements or high volatility periods

ADDITIONAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- Monitor for false signals during sideways/choppy markets
- Consider using volume confirmation to validate crossover signals
- Backtest strategy performance across different market conditions
- Maintain trading journal to track performance and refine approach
- Be aware of whipsaws during periods of low volatility
