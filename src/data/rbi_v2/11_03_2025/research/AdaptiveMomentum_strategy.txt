STRATEGY_NAME: Adaptive Momentum

STRATEGY_DETAILS:
This strategy uses the 20-period and 200-period Exponential Moving Average (EMA) crossover on SPY hourly charts to identify significant trend changes and momentum shifts in the market.

KEY STRATEGY COMPONENTS:
- Primary Instrument: SPY (SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust)
- Timeframe: 1-hour charts
- Core Indicators: 20-period EMA (fast) and 200-period EMA (slow)
- Trend Identification: Uses the relationship between short-term and long-term moving averages

ENTRY RULES:
LONG ENTRY:
- When the 20 EMA crosses ABOVE the 200 EMA
- Enter on the close of the hourly candle where crossover occurs
- Confirm with price action showing bullish momentum

SHORT ENTRY:
- When the 20 EMA crosses BELOW the 200 EMA
- Enter on the close of the hourly candle where crossover occurs
- Confirm with price action showing bearish momentum

EXIT RULES:
LONG EXIT:
- When the 20 EMA crosses back below the 200 EMA
- Exit on the close of the hourly candle where crossover occurs
- Consider partial profit-taking if price deviates significantly from the 20 EMA

SHORT EXIT:
- When the 20 EMA crosses back above the 200 EMA
- Exit on the close of the hourly candle where crossover occurs
- Consider partial profit-taking if price deviates significantly from the 20 EMA

RISK MANAGEMENT:
- Position sizing: Risk no more than 1-2% of account per trade
- Stop loss: Place 2-3% below entry for long positions, 2-3% above entry for short positions
- Trailing stops: Consider moving stop to breakeven once trade moves 2-3% in your favor
- Maximum drawdown: Limit to 5-8% of total account value

REQUIRED INDICATORS:
1. 20-period Exponential Moving Average (EMA)
2. 200-period Exponential Moving Average (EMA)
3. Optional: Volume confirmation to validate crossover strength
4. Optional: RSI (14) to identify overbought/oversold conditions for entry timing

ADDITIONAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- This strategy works best in trending markets, may produce false signals in ranging markets
- Consider filtering signals during major economic news events
- Monitor overall market conditions and sector rotation for confirmation
- Backtest thoroughly to understand typical holding periods and win rates
